# Task 1: Infographic Description

## Tool Used
Canva (Free Version)

## Design Overview
Created a single-page vertical infographic (A4 size) titled "Digital Literacy: Your Guide to the Online World" with a blue and white color scheme.

## Content Sections

### Section 1: What is Digital Literacy?
Visual: Lightbulb icon with digital elements
Text: "More than just using a computer—it's the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information safely using digital technologies."

### Section 2: Safe Internet Practices
Visual: Shield icon with checkmarks
Bullet points:
- Use strong, unique passwords for each account
- Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible
- Don't click suspicious links or download unknown attachments
- Keep software and apps updated

### Section 3: Professional Online Presence
Visual: Person icon with upward graph
Bullet points:
- Clean up your social media before job applications
- Create a LinkedIn profile even as a student
- Share relevant content related to your field
- Google yourself regularly to monitor your digital footprint

## Reflection on Creation Process
I spent about 2 hours on this because I kept changing the layout. The hardest part was making sure the text was readable when exported as a PNG. I initially used a fancy font that looked terrible when compressed, so I switched to basic sans-serif fonts. Also realized I can't design for too many topics at once—three was the sweet spot for one page. The Canva template helped a lot but I customized the colors to look more "tech" and less "corporate brochure."
