[SECTION 1: WHAT IS DIGITAL LITERACY?]

Digital literacy means:
→ Finding information online effectively
→ Evaluating if sources are trustworthy  
→ Creating content responsibly
→ Communicating safely across platforms
→ Protecting your privacy and data

It's not just technical skills—it's critical thinking in digital spaces.

[SECTION 2: SAFE INTERNET PRACTICES FOR STUDENTS]

🔒 PASSWORD SAFETY
• Use different passwords for each account
• Mix letters, numbers, and symbols
• Consider a password manager

🛡️ TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION  
• Always enable 2FA on banking apps
• Use authenticator apps instead of SMS when possible

⚠️ PHISHING AWARENESS
• Check sender email addresses carefully
• Banks never ask for OTPs or PINs
• When in doubt, don't click

🔄 REGULAR UPDATES
• Keep your phone and laptop OS updated
• Update apps to patch security holes

[SECTION 3: BUILDING PROFESSIONAL PRESENCE]

WHY IT MATTERS:
Recruiters Google you before interviews. Make sure they find good things.

STUDENT CHECKLIST:
✓ Create LinkedIn profile (add your college, year, skills)
✓ Make GitHub repository for coding projects
✓ Remove questionable content from Instagram/Facebook
✓ Use professional email (yourname@college.edu or Gmail)
✓ Join relevant groups in your field

REMEMBER: The internet never forgets. Post accordingly.
