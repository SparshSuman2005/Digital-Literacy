# Task 3 Part B: Google Form

## Form Title
Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz for VIT Students

## Purpose
To assess how much my batchmates know about digital safety and professional online behavior. Could be used as part of Digital Ambassador activities to identify knowledge gaps.

## Form Questions

### Question 1 (Multiple Choice)
**Question:** You receive an email from "vit-bhopal-support@gmail.com" saying your university password will expire in 24 hours and you must click a link to reset it. What should you do?

**Options:**
- A) Click the link immediately and enter my current password to verify
- B) Forward the email to friends so they're aware too
- C) Check if the sender is actually from VIT Bhopal (official emails end with @vitbhopal.ac.in) and report it to IT if suspicious
- D) Ignore it completely without checking

**Correct Answer:** C

### Question 2 (Short Answer)
**Question:** Describe what you would do if you accidentally clicked on a suspicious link that asked for your banking UPI PIN.

**Expected Good Answer:**
Immediately close the page, don't enter any information, check if any transaction happened in my bank account, and report the phishing attempt to the bank and cybercrime.gov.in. Change passwords if I entered any.

### Question 3 (Multiple Choice - Checkbox)
**Question:** Which of the following are signs of a professional online presence? (Select all that apply)

**Options:**
- Having a LinkedIn profile with updated education details
- Using the same casual username across all platforms (like "cool_dude_99")
- Regularly posting about industry trends and learnings
- Keeping Instagram public with all personal photos visible to everyone

**Correct Answers:** A and C

### Question 4 (Short Answer)
**Question:** Why is it recommended to use different passwords for your college email and social media accounts?

**Expected Good Answer:**
If one account gets hacked, the attacker can't access all other accounts using the same password. It's called credential stuffing and prevents total digital identity theft.

### Question 5 (Linear Scale)
**Question:** On a scale of 1-5, how confident are you in identifying phishing emails? (1 = Not confident at all, 5 = Very confident)

**Scale:** 1 to 5
