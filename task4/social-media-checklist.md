# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## 5 Do's ✅

1. **Keep Professional and Personal Accounts Separate**
   - Use LinkedIn for career-related content only
   - Keep Facebook/Instagram for personal life but ensure privacy settings are tight

2. **Think Before Posting**
   - Wait 10 minutes before posting anything emotional or controversial
   - Ask yourself: "Would I want a future employer to see this?"

3. **Use Privacy Settings Wisely**
   - Regularly review who can see your posts
   - Limit location tagging to close friends only
   - Keep contact details private

4. **Share Industry-Related Content**
   - Post about tech trends if you're in CS/AI
   - Share interesting articles with your own brief thoughts
   - This shows you're engaged with your field

5. **Google Yourself Monthly**
   - Check what comes up when someone searches your name
   - Clean up any old content that doesn't represent who you are now
   - Set up Google Alerts for your name

## 5 Don'ts ❌

1. **Don't Share Sensitive Personal Information**
   - Never post your address, phone number, or financial details
   - Don't share screenshots of bank transactions even if blurred
   - Avoid posting when you're home alone or traveling alone in real-time

2. **Don't Post When Angry or Emotional**
   - Ranting about professors, college administration, or employers publicly can backfire
   - Emotional posts seem like a good idea at the moment but create permanent records

3. **Don't Engage in Online Arguments**
   - Comment wars solve nothing and make you look unprofessional
   - If someone is trolling, block and move on—don't feed the drama

4. **Don't Accept Friend Requests from Unknown People Blindly**
   - Verify who they are before accepting
   - Fake profiles are common—check mutual friends and profile authenticity
   - Don't click links sent by new connections immediately

5. **Don't Mix Professional and Unprofessional Content**
   - Don't share party photos on LinkedIn
   - Don't complain about your internship/job on Twitter where employers can see
   - Keep the context appropriate for the platform
