# Task 5 Reflection: Cybercrime Awareness

I chose UPI fraud because I've actually experienced something similar. Last year, I got a message saying I won a prize and needed to pay a small "processing fee" via UPI to claim it. I almost clicked the link but something felt off—the grammar was weird and the urgency was too pushy. I ignored it and later found out it was a common scam. Researching this topic for the assignment showed me exactly how close I came to losing money.

What surprised me most was how sophisticated these scams have become. I used to think only gullible people fall for fraud, but now I see they use psychology—fear of missing out, fear of service disconnection, fake urgency—to make you act without thinking. The fake websites look exactly like real Paytm or Google Pay screens. Even smart people can get caught if they're in a hurry or stressed.

One habit I'm changing: I never checked my bank statements regularly before. Maybe once a month if I remembered. Now I check every Sunday evening for 5 minutes. I also installed the Cyber Crime reporting app and saved the 1930 helpline number in my phone contacts—not just memorized it, actually saved it so I don't have to search if something happens suddenly.

This task made me realize digital safety isn't a one-time setup (like installing an antivirus). It's a constant awareness during every transaction. As a Digital Ambassador, I can actually help my friends by spotting red flags in messages they receive and showing them how to report incidents properly.
