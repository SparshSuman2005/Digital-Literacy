# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

## Essential Safety Tips

1. **Always Check the URL Carefully**
   - Real sites use HTTPS and correct spellings (paytm.com, not paytm-bill.in)
   - Look for lock icon in browser before entering any payment details
   - When in doubt, manually type the website address instead of clicking links

2. **Never Share UPI PIN or OTP**
   - Banks and UPI apps never ask for PIN over phone, email, or messages
   - UPI PIN is only entered on your device, never on a website or shared verbally
   - If someone asks for OTP "to verify" or "to refund," it's a scam

3. **Verify Urgency Claims Independently**
   - If someone says "account will be blocked" or "power cut in 2 hours," hang up and check directly
   - Call official customer care numbers from bank statements, not from messages received
   - Real emergencies don't require immediate payment without verification

4. **Use Official Apps Only**
   - Download banking/UPI apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store
   - Check app developer name and reviews before installing
   - Keep apps updated to latest security patches

5. **Enable Transaction Limits and Alerts**
   - Set daily transaction limits in your UPI app (₹5,000-10,000 max for daily use)
   - Enable SMS and email alerts for every transaction
   - Check bank statements weekly, not just monthly

6. **Financial Safety: Verify Before Paying Unknown Numbers**
   - Always confirm the recipient's identity when paying new phone numbers via UPI
   - Use "Verify UPI ID" feature before sending money to new contacts
   - Never pay "advance fees" to claim prizes or lottery winnings—legitimate prizes don't require payment

7. **Financial Safety: Secure Your UPI Apps**
   - Use app lock (PIN/Fingerprint) for all payment apps
   - Don't save UPI PIN in password managers or notes apps
   - If you lose your phone, immediately call bank to block UPI services (keep customer care numbers written down, not just in phone)

8. **Report Suspicious Activity Immediately**
   - **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
   - **24/7 Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (Call immediately if money is deducted fraudulently)
   - Contact your bank's customer care to block cards/UPI if compromised
   - File FIR at local police station for amounts above ₹50,000
